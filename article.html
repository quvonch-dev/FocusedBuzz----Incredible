7 Tips To Make Your Home Look Luxurious

By Robert Bailey · June 12, 2025 · 5 min read

In an ideal world, we'd all have access to a skilled interior designer who could assist us in creating spaces that exude comfort, luxury, and refinement. Fortunately, there are methods to get a luxurious design without going broke.

1. Invest in Quality Lighting

Lighting is one of the most transformative elements in any room. Replace harsh overhead lights with layered lighting — a combination of ambient, task, and accent lighting. Dimmer switches give you control over the mood, while statement chandeliers or sculptural pendants add instant drama.

2. Add Texture With Soft Furnishings

Luxury is often about how a space feels, not just how it looks. Layer different textures — velvet throw pillows, linen curtains, a chunky knit blanket — to create a rich, tactile environment. The key is to mix materials that complement each other.

3. Use a Neutral Color Palette

High-end interiors often rely on a restrained, neutral palette with pops of color through accessories. Think creams, taupes, warm greys, and soft whites. This doesn't mean boring — within these tones you can play with depth and contrast.

4. Declutter Ruthlessly

Nothing undermines luxury faster than clutter. Luxury spaces feel spacious and intentional. Invest in stylish storage solutions to keep surfaces clear, and curate your decorative items so that each one earns its place.

5. Upgrade Your Hardware

Swapping out standard cabinet pulls, door handles, and faucets for premium alternatives is a small change with a big impact. Brushed gold, matte black, or polished nickel hardware instantly elevates kitchens and bathrooms.

6. Hang Artwork at the Right Height

The gallery standard is to hang artwork so the center of the piece is at eye level — roughly 57 to 60 inches from the floor. Group multiple pieces in a cohesive gallery wall for a collected, curated look.

7. Bring Nature Inside

Fresh flowers and well-chosen plants breathe life into a room and signal that someone cares about the space. A single dramatic plant — a fiddle-leaf fig, a monstera — can anchor a living room and add a sculptural quality that no piece of furniture can replicate.
